Cleansheet Platform

Privacy Principles

At Cleansheet, we believe education should never require surrendering your privacy. In an era where platforms treat user data as currency, we've chosen a different path. These principles guide how we handle your data, design our platform, and run our business.

Core Principles

1. Data Ownership

Your educational content, projects, and progress belong to you.

  • We don't claim ownership of your work
  • Export your data anytime in standard formats
  • Account deletion removes your data within 90 days
  • Clear terms with no hidden exceptions

2. Data Minimization

We collect only what's necessary to deliver our educational services.

Data we collect:

  • Account information (email, name)
  • Learning progress and activity
  • Platform preferences and settings
  • Service usage patterns (anonymized)

Data we don't collect:

  • Browsing history outside our platform
  • Social network connections
  • Detailed location tracking
  • Cross-site behavioral data

3. No Advertising Model

Our revenue comes from subscriptions and services, not advertising.

  • No banner ads or sponsored content
  • No third-party tracking pixels
  • No behavioral targeting networks
  • No data sales to advertisers

4. Responsible AI Use

AI features enhance learning without exploiting user data.

  • Models trained on reviewed public content, not user submissions
  • User content never used for commercial model training
  • AI interactions remain private
  • Opt-in approach for AI-powered features

5. Privacy-Focused Analytics

We measure platform performance, not individual behavior.

  • Aggregate usage patterns for feature improvement
  • Performance metrics for platform reliability
  • Error tracking for bug resolution
  • Anonymous feedback for quality enhancement

6. Transparency & Accountability

Our practices are documented and publicly accessible.

  • Privacy policy maintained in public repository
  • Security practices openly documented
  • Clear communication of data handling procedures
  • Advance notice of policy changes (90 days)
  • Regular third-party security audits

Implementation

For Learners

  • Focus on learning without surveillance concerns
  • Develop portfolios with full ownership rights
  • Engage with AI tools privately
  • Control your data through export and deletion options
  • Learn at your own pace without algorithmic manipulation

For Coaches

  • Collaborate with learners in a secure environment
  • Retain intellectual property rights to your curriculum
  • Receive fair compensation (70% revenue share)
  • Build meaningful relationships without surveillance
  • Maintain professional privacy standards

For Organizations

  • White-label deployments with privacy-first architecture
  • Custom integrations respecting data boundaries
  • Enterprise-grade security standards
  • Transparent service level agreements
  • Audit-ready compliance documentation

Privacy Commitments

Operational Standards

  • No sale or rental of user data
  • User content not used for AI training without explicit consent
  • Ad-free platform model maintained
  • 90-day advance notice for material policy changes
  • User notification of data requests when legally permitted
  • Data deletion within 90 days of account closure
  • Strong encryption for data in transit and at rest
  • Regular third-party security audits
  • Data collection limited to service requirements
  • User-first design principles (no dark patterns)

Accountability Protocol

In the event of material changes to these principles:

  • Immediate public announcement with clear explanation
  • 90-day notice period before implementation
  • Simplified data export and account closure process
  • Transparent communication in plain language

Revenue Model

Sustainable Privacy-First Approach

Our revenue comes from delivering value, not exploiting data.

  • Subscription Services: Tiered platform access (Member, Learner, Seeker tiers)
  • Coaching Services: 30/70 revenue split (platform/coach)
  • Enterprise Solutions: Custom deployments and integrations
  • AI Pipeline Services: Content curation and processing services

What we exclude:

  • Data sales to third parties
  • Advertising revenue streams
  • Behavioral data monetization
  • Privacy-compromising free tiers

Technical Implementation

Security Architecture

  • Encryption: TLS/SSL in transit, encryption at rest for stored data
  • Authentication: Bcrypt password hashing, multi-factor authentication support
  • Access Control: Role-based permissions with least privilege principle
  • Session Management: Automatic timeouts with security monitoring

Data Handling

  • Purpose-limited collection practices
  • Immediate anonymization where applicable
  • Automated deletion per retention schedules
  • Regular data audits and cleanup procedures

Infrastructure

  • Cloud Platform: Azure (Microsoft enterprise security standards)
  • Redundancy: Reliability-focused, not data multiplication
  • Backups: Encrypted with strict access controls
  • Distribution: Performance optimization, minimal data replication

Platform Maturity

Current Development Stage

Cleansheet is in active development with continuous feature deployment.

Current state:

  • Rapid iteration and feature releases
  • Best-effort security implementation
  • Ongoing refinement of user experience
  • Regular platform updates

Consistent standards:

  • Privacy principles remain constant
  • Data protection standards maintained
  • Core commitments not subject to change

  • Avoid submitting highly sensitive personal data (SSN, financial accounts, medical records)

Social Responsibility

Serving Underserved Communities

We allocate 20% of profits to underserved community access because privacy and educational opportunity are interconnected.

  • Equal privacy standards regardless of payment tier
  • No surveillance-based "free" alternatives
  • Protection considerations for vulnerable populations
  • Technology as empowerment, not exploitation

Enhanced protections:

  • Minimal location data collection
  • Protected identity information handling
  • Bias-aware system design
  • Threat-aware security measures for at-risk users

Legal Compliance

Data Disclosure Standards

We disclose user data only when legally required:

  • Valid court orders with proper jurisdiction
  • Imminent harm prevention scenarios
  • Unavoidable government requests

Our response protocol:

  • Legal review and challenge of questionable requests
  • User notification when legally permissible
  • Annual transparency report publication
  • Legal defense of user privacy rights
  • No voluntary participation in mass surveillance programs

Continuous Improvement

Privacy-First Growth

As the platform evolves, privacy principles guide all development decisions.

  • Privacy-by-design methodology for new features
  • Privacy assessment for all partnerships
  • Revenue diversification without data monetization
  • Security enhancements with each platform update

Change management:

  • Public announcement of material policy changes
  • Clear rationale documentation
  • 90-day implementation timeline
  • Streamlined data export and account closure

Accountability mechanisms:

  • Public repository hosting all policy documentation
  • Annual third-party security and privacy audits
  • User advisory board (in development)
  • Direct privacy concern reporting channels

Summary

Cleansheet provides career development education through a privacy-first platform model.

These principles are operational commitments, not marketing statements.

We succeed by delivering value to users, not by exploiting their data.

Privacy is our operational foundation, not an optional feature.
