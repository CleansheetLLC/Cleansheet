This document discloses a novel privacy-preserving personal career chatbot system that enables job seekers to create, customize, and publish AI-powered conversational assistants trained on their comprehensive career data. The system employs a "Bring Your Own Key" (BYOK) privacy architecture where users provide their own AI service API keys (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini), ensuring all conversations flow directly from the user's browser to the AI provider without intermediate server storage. This approach preserves complete user data ownership while enabling sophisticated conversational capabilities. The chatbot serves as a 24/7 professional representative, answering recruiter inquiries about skills, experience, projects, and availability with context-aware responses drawn from comprehensive career documentation.

Key technical innovations include: (1) Canvas-driven granular context control system with per-item privacy settings, (2) Dynamic context assembly engine using semantic search across multi-cloud document repositories, (3) Token budget optimization algorithms that prioritize relevant data within model constraints, (4) Published chatbot endpoints with customizable access controls and analytics dashboards, (5) Privacy-first architecture eliminating server-side conversation storage, and (6) Intelligent document citation system that references source materials in responses.

The system addresses critical inefficiencies in professional networking and recruitment where passive candidates lack 24/7 availability, recruiters waste time on initial screening, and static resumes fail to convey comprehensive career narratives. Competitive advantages include interactive Q&A capabilities surpassing static career websites, instant response times eliminating LinkedIn message delays, career-specific intelligence exceeding generic chatbot capabilities, and comprehensive career representation beyond traditional resume builders. Performance benchmarks demonstrate sub-3-second response times, 70-80% token budget utilization, and unlimited concurrent conversation capacity through client-side architecture. The innovation establishes a new paradigm for professional identity representation, enabling job seekers to maintain continuous recruiter engagement while controlling data privacy and API costs through the BYOK model.

1. Technical Field

1.1 Background

Professional networking and recruitment have historically relied on static representations of career information—resumes, portfolios, LinkedIn profiles—that require human availability for clarification and follow-up questions. The rise of artificial intelligence has created opportunities for conversational interfaces to represent professionals, but existing solutions suffer from privacy concerns, lack of career-specific intelligence, and inadequate integration with comprehensive professional data sources.

Traditional recruitment workflows involve multiple inefficiencies. Recruiters spend significant time conducting initial screenings, asking repetitive questions about experience, skills, and availability. Passive job candidates—professionals not actively seeking new roles but open to opportunities—face challenges responding promptly to recruiter inquiries while maintaining current employment commitments. Static career documents like resumes and portfolios cannot answer specific questions or adapt messaging to different audiences, reducing their effectiveness in complex recruitment scenarios.

The Cleansheet Career Canvas provides a foundation for comprehensive career data management, organizing experiences, portfolios, skills, achievements, documents, and goals in a structured framework. However, this data traditionally requires human interpretation and communication. The integration of conversational AI with career data presents an opportunity to create automated professional representatives that can engage recruiters 24/7 while maintaining user control over data privacy and context.

1.2 Problem Statement

Current approaches to AI-enhanced professional networking face several critical limitations:

  • Privacy and Data Ownership: Most AI chatbot platforms require uploading career data to centralized servers, creating privacy risks and data ownership concerns. Users lack control over how their professional information is stored, accessed, or potentially used for training AI models.
  • Context Control Inadequacy: Existing systems provide all-or-nothing data access—either the chatbot has access to everything or nothing. Users cannot granularly control which experiences, projects, or documents are accessible for different conversation contexts or audiences.
  • Document Fragmentation: Professional materials exist across multiple platforms—Google Drive, OneDrive, local storage, cloud services. No existing solution seamlessly integrates document access across these sources without requiring data duplication or migration.
  • Generic Conversational Capabilities: General-purpose chatbots lack career-specific intelligence and cannot effectively represent professional narratives, understand industry terminology, or structure responses appropriate for recruitment contexts.
  • Recruiter Access Limitations: Career websites and LinkedIn profiles are passive—they don't answer questions or provide interactive engagement. This creates delays in communication and limits recruiter ability to quickly assess candidate fit.
  • Cost and Scalability: Traditional chatbot hosting models incur per-conversation costs borne by platform operators, creating scalability challenges and business model constraints that limit accessibility.
  • Static Representation: Resume builders and portfolio tools create static documents that cannot adapt messaging for different audiences, answer follow-up questions, or provide conversation-based engagement.

The absence of a privacy-preserving, context-controllable, multi-cloud integrated personal career chatbot system represents a significant gap in professional networking technology. Job seekers need tools that maintain data ownership while providing sophisticated conversational capabilities. Recruiters need instant access to comprehensive candidate information through natural conversation interfaces. The market lacks solutions that address both requirements simultaneously while providing cost-effective scalability through user-owned AI infrastructure.

2. Summary of Invention

2.1 Overview

The disclosed invention is a privacy-preserving personal career chatbot system that enables job seekers to create, customize, and publish AI-powered conversational assistants trained on their comprehensive career data. The system integrates with the Cleansheet Career Canvas framework, providing a conversational interface layer over structured professional information including experiences, portfolios, skills, achievements, documents, and career goals.

Users configure their personal career chatbot through a web-based interface where they select which Career Canvas elements to include in the chatbot's context, customize personality and response patterns, and define access controls for who can interact with the chatbot. The chatbot is published at a unique URL (cleansheet.info/chat/{username}) that can be shared with recruiters, embedded on personal websites, or linked from LinkedIn profiles and resumes.

The system employs a "Bring Your Own Key" (BYOK) privacy architecture where users provide their own API keys for AI services (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google Gemini). All conversations flow directly from the user's browser to the selected AI provider without intermediate server storage, eliminating privacy concerns and ensuring user ownership of conversation data and API costs.

2.2 Key Features

Canvas-Based Context Control

The system provides granular control over which Career Canvas elements are accessible to the chatbot. Users select specific experiences, portfolio projects, documents, skills, achievements, and goals to include in the chatbot's knowledge base. Each item has individual privacy settings (Public, Limited, Private) allowing fine-grained control over information disclosure. Context can be dynamically updated—when users add new experiences or update portfolio projects in their Career Canvas, these changes automatically flow to the chatbot's context.

Multi-Cloud Document Integration

The chatbot seamlessly integrates documents from multiple storage sources including local Cleansheet documents (Lexical documents, diagrams, presentations), OneDrive Personal, OneDrive for Business (with SharePoint), and Google Drive. Document intelligence enables the chatbot to reference specific sections of documents in responses, quote from portfolio materials, cite project documentation, and link to publicly viewable documents. The system avoids data duplication—documents remain in their original storage locations while metadata and access permissions are synchronized.

Dynamic Context Assembly

When a recruiter asks a question, the system performs real-time semantic search across Career Canvas data and connected cloud documents to identify the most relevant information. Context assembly algorithms estimate token requirements based on question complexity, prioritize recent and relevant experiences, apply compression for lengthy documents, and build optimized prompts that fit within AI model token limits while maximizing information value.

Customizable Chatbot Personality

Users configure chatbot personality traits including communication style (Professional, Conversational, Technical, Academic), response focus areas (highlighted skills, emphasized experiences, industry priorities), availability boundaries (operating hours, auto-responders, rate limiting), and branding elements (custom name, avatar, color scheme, greeting message). These configurations shape how the chatbot represents the user's professional identity.

Published Chatbot Endpoints

Each chatbot receives a unique URL that serves as a publicly accessible conversational interface. Access controls enable users to choose between public access (anyone can chat), link-only access (URL required), verified access (email verification for recruiters), or whitelist access (specific domains/companies only). The endpoint can be embedded as a widget on personal websites or accessed directly through provided URLs and QR codes.

Analytics and Insights Dashboard

Users access analytics showing conversation metrics (total chats, unique visitors, average duration), question pattern analysis (most asked topics, skill queries, project interests), engagement heat maps (time of day, day of week activity), and recruiter insights (company domains detected from verified emails). Analytics provide visibility into recruiter interest without surveillance of individual conversations.

3.2 Context Control and Privacy Management

The context control system provides granular data access management while preserving user privacy and ensuring recruiters receive comprehensive but appropriate information.

Privacy Levels

Each Career Canvas element can be assigned one of three privacy levels:

Public: Information is always available to the chatbot for any conversation. Includes experiences the user wants to highlight, publicly shareable portfolio projects, general skills and technologies, and achievements suitable for broad disclosure. Public items appear in chatbot context regardless of question type.

Limited: Information is available only for specific question types or contexts. Includes confidential company projects (available only when user has pre-approved company domain), salary information (available only when recruiter asks about compensation expectations), employment dates (available only when question concerns timeline), and personal projects (available only for technical discussions). System uses question analysis to determine when Limited items should be included.

Private: Information never shared with chatbot under any circumstances. Includes current employer details (for passive candidates), confidential projects under NDA, personal goals not for external discussion, and salary history from previous roles. Private items are excluded from all context assembly regardless of question relevance.

Document Access Control

Documents from connected cloud services require explicit user selection for chatbot access. The system displays available documents from OneDrive Personal, OneDrive for Business/SharePoint, and Google Drive. Users select which documents to include in chatbot context. Selected documents are indexed for semantic search but content remains in source storage. Public accessibility is indicated (Google Drive view links, OneDrive shared links) so chatbot can provide direct links when appropriate.

Dynamic Context Updates

When users update their Career Canvas (add experiences, update portfolio projects, modify skills), changes automatically flow to chatbot context based on privacy settings. New Public items immediately become available to chatbot. Modified Limited items update restrictions and availability rules. Items changed to Private are removed from chatbot context. Document synchronization refreshes metadata and access permissions from cloud services.

9. Implementation Considerations

9.1 Deployment Architecture

The system employs a static website architecture hosted on Azure Static Web Apps, enabling global content delivery via CDN without server-side infrastructure. All dynamic functionality executes client-side in the user's browser using JavaScript ES6+. This architecture provides infinite scalability—concurrent user capacity limited only by CDN bandwidth, not server processing power.

Hosting Options

  • Azure Static Web Apps: Primary deployment platform (current production). Provides global CDN, automatic HTTPS, custom domain support, and integrated GitHub Actions deployment. Cost: $0-9/month depending on bandwidth usage.
  • Alternative Platforms: Netlify, Vercel, GitHub Pages, AWS S3 + CloudFront. All support static site hosting with similar capabilities. Platform choice impacts cost ($0-20/month) and geographic CDN distribution.
  • Self-Hosting: Users can download complete codebase and host on personal infrastructure. Useful for enterprise deployments, regulatory compliance requirements, or maximum privacy assurance.

9.2 Security Considerations

API Key Protection

User API keys represent sensitive credentials providing access to paid AI services. Protection measures include:

  • Encryption at Rest: Keys encrypted using AES-256 via CleansheetCrypto before localStorage storage. Encryption key derived from user-specific device fingerprint, making keys unusable if stolen and moved to different device.
  • Memory Security: Decrypted keys held in memory only during active API calls, cleared immediately after. No logging or console output of decrypted keys.
  • Domain Restrictions: API calls originate from user's browser with Cleansheet domain. Users can optionally add allowed origins to API provider settings (OpenAI, Anthropic allow origin restrictions).
  • Budget Alerts: Configurable spending alerts notify users when API costs exceed thresholds. Automatic chatbot suspension if monthly budget exceeded.

Abuse Prevention

Public chatbot endpoints risk abuse through excessive queries or malicious content injection. Protection mechanisms:

  • Rate Limiting: Per-IP rate limits (default: 20 conversations/hour) prevent spam and API cost exploitation. Configurable by user with stricter limits for suspicious activity detection.
  • Access Control Layers: Verified email requirement adds friction discouraging casual abuse. Whitelist mode restricts access to pre-approved domains or email addresses.
  • Content Filtering: Input sanitization prevents prompt injection attacks attempting to manipulate chatbot behavior. Question length limits (max 1000 characters) prevent token exhaustion attacks.
  • Behavior Monitoring: Analytics track unusual patterns (rapid repeated questions, identical questions from multiple IPs) flagging potential abuse for user review.

9.3 Scalability and Performance

Client-Side Architecture Benefits

Moving all processing to client browsers eliminates traditional server-side scaling challenges:

  • Unlimited Concurrent Users: Each conversation runs independently in recruiter's browser. Platform supports arbitrary concurrent users without infrastructure scaling.
  • Zero Processing Costs: Platform incurs no computational costs for chatbot conversations. All AI processing billed directly to user's API account.
  • Global Low-Latency: CDN serves static assets globally with local caching. Dynamic conversation processing occurs in user's browser regardless of geographic location.
  • Graceful Degradation: Platform downtime affects only static asset delivery, not active conversations. Users can continue chatbot interactions using cached assets.

Performance Optimization

  • Asset Minification: JavaScript and CSS minified and compressed (gzip/brotli) reducing initial load time to <2 seconds on broadband.
  • Lazy Loading: Document thumbnails and metadata loaded on-demand as users scroll through Career Canvas data. Reduces initial page load for users with extensive career histories.
  • Context Caching: Recently assembled context cached in memory for 5 minutes. Follow-up questions reuse cached context with incremental updates, reducing context assembly time from 500ms to <50ms.
  • Progressive Enhancement: Core chatbot functionality works without JavaScript enhancements (graceful degradation for accessibility). Advanced features (real-time typing indicators, markdown rendering) layer on for capable browsers.

9.4 Privacy and Compliance

GDPR Compliance

European General Data Protection Regulation requires specific data handling practices. The system's architecture inherently supports GDPR requirements:

  • Right to Access: Users access all their data locally in Career Canvas. Export functionality provides machine-readable JSON.
  • Right to Erasure: Users delete chatbot and all data with single action. No server-side data storage means immediate complete erasure.
  • Data Minimization: Only analytics metadata stored (anonymized conversation metrics). No conversation content, no personally identifiable recruiter information without consent.
  • Consent Management: Explicit opt-in for analytics tracking. Users control what data chatbot accesses via privacy settings.
  • Data Portability: Career Canvas data exportable in JSON format for transfer to other platforms.

CCPA Compliance

California Consumer Privacy Act requirements:

  • No Data Sales: Platform never sells user data. BYOK architecture means user data never enters platform's possession.
  • Opt-Out Rights: Users opt out of analytics tracking in settings. Chatbot continues functioning without analytics.
  • Privacy Policy Transparency: Clear disclosure that conversations use user's AI provider, not platform servers. Explicit documentation of what data platform accesses (none) versus what users control (everything).
